Epic Games

I was a Senior Tester for the Unreal Engine 5, specializing in testing Tools such as: Modeling, Terrain, Foliage, Water, UV Editor, DaySequence, World Partition, Data Layers, Geometry Script, and PCG.

I started at Epic Games as a Tester 1 and worked my way up the 3 years I was there to a Senior Tester. Since the start, I was placed on a team that focused on the tools above, running test cases and experimental testing to make sure the tool was running as expected for end users. If not, I was to write a bug and report the issue, as well as verify bugs in my field to ensure fixes were optimal and no additional issues arose.

Roblox Studio Lessons

These small projects are some of the few things that I have worked with students on creating. I was in charge of teaching them how to create levels within Roblox Studio, and in some cases coding within Lua.

Snowy Campsite

This is a rendered scene I did in Unreal Engine 5 using Fab assets. Wanting to focus more on level design rather than modeling, as well as a learning experience to grow my skills.

I created a small landscape with a layered landscape material to allow painted layers, which I knew the basics of, but making the material itself was a new challenge. I started with just a material on the landscape to have a starting point, and once I had all the assets down, I created the layered landscape material to paint the areas I deemed necessary.

Another challenge I learned from was using Runtime Virtual Textures to blend the materials on objects with the layered landscape material I made. However, once I was able to find a solution, the result was presicely what I needed.

Darkness Eternal

Darkness Eternal was my college final, where I joined 6 other on campus students to create the group Dyslexia Untied. Within this group, we created this within Unreal Engine 4 and we had 4 months to complete this project, with a milestone each month for each class. At first, we were on campus and meeting almost daily. However, this was the beginning of 2020, and unfortunately COVID-19 hit and the school shut down, forcing us all online.

My work within this project mostly consists of constructing the Tutorial, the Tutorial Level, as well as writing design for the enemy introductions, Tool Tips, and the stats menu for the towers. It doesn't seem like much, but the tutorial continues into almost every level that introduces new zombies, but even the stats menu had to revamp the upgrades. This was a difficult process to do, as the upgrades were hard-coded, and in order for the numbers to appear correctly, I had to find a way to make them modular.

In the end, we had a final product. With a collaboration from all of us, we were able to fund and publish this game to Steam, with our Team Lead being the Producer and uploading the final product.

SCRAPS Level

This was a college project that spanned three months and three classes teaching us about level design. We used Unity, with the ProBuilder plugin as our tools, and using free PBR textures. Our professors wanted us to focus primarily on level design, and gave us already made mechanics to implement while creating our levels.

During the first month, this was focused solely on creating documentation to start our levels. This included a GDD implementing our research and theme to our level, a bubble diagram to focus on components and setup, and then drawing out the level look and placing the mechanics.

The second month was starting to work on the Alpha setup within Unity, blocking out everything and making things to scale. This included modeling everything that we needed in the level ourselves minus the mechanics given to us. We also had to video the walkthrough, as shown in the slide.

The third and final month was where we had to go through Beta and Gold, as well as joining into a group and merging all of our levels together. I was joined by 5 of my classmates (3 on campus, 2 online), where we teamed together to create our levels. Providing feedback to one another, we banded together to produce our line of levels and finished off the three months of hard work.

Picnic Table Model

This is a rendered scene I did within Maya of a handmade picnic table and fence. This is a project I did within college, learning how to model as well as using materials and editing the UVs.

I had basic 3D modeling knowledge from learning 3DSMax in highschool. However, this class continued to grow my skills and the final week piece that I created was this picnic table. We were told to make it as detailed as possible, and I was the only one who made this type of table to challenge myself.

What the images don't show is that there are detailed nails within the fence and table, as well as no z-fighting and each piece of wood are modeled like you would cut the pieces of wood. Only the tree and grass were not modeled by me, as they were given to us for use for the class.
